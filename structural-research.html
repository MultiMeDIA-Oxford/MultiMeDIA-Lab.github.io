Structural Heart Research

Advanced analysis of heart anatomy, chamber geometry, and tissue architecture using multimodal imaging techniques

Research Overview

Our structural heart research focuses on understanding the complex three-dimensional anatomy of the heart using cutting-edge AI and machine learning techniques. We develop advanced methods for analyzing cardiac structure from various imaging modalities to improve diagnosis and treatment of structural heart diseases.

Key Research Areas

3D Cardiac Anatomy Reconstruction

Developing algorithms to create detailed three-dimensional models of cardiac structures from medical imaging data, enabling better visualization and analysis of heart anatomy.

Chamber Geometry Analysis

Quantitative assessment of cardiac chamber shapes, volumes, and morphological features to identify pathological changes and predict clinical outcomes.

Tissue Architecture Characterization

Advanced analysis of myocardial tissue structure and organization using high-resolution imaging techniques and computational methods.

Morphological Feature Extraction

Automated identification and quantification of important structural features for disease diagnosis and risk stratification.

Current Projects

AI-Driven Cardiac Segmentation

Developing deep learning models for automatic and accurate segmentation of cardiac structures from various imaging modalities including MRI, CT, and echocardiography.

Status: Active

Multi-Modal Imaging Fusion

Creating integrated analysis pipelines that combine information from multiple imaging modalities to provide comprehensive structural assessment.

Status: Active

Automated Anatomical Measurements

Building tools for standardized, reproducible measurement of cardiac dimensions and structural parameters from medical images.

Status: Development
