Mechanical Heart Research

Study of cardiac mechanics, contractility, strain analysis, and biomechanical properties of heart tissue using advanced computational modeling and AI-driven analysis.

Contributor(s)

Thalia Seale

Research Focus Areas

Cardiac Contractility Analysis

Quantitative assessment of myocardial contractile function, including systolic and diastolic performance evaluation using advanced imaging and computational methods.

Strain and Stress Measurements

Regional and global strain analysis to evaluate cardiac deformation patterns and mechanical properties during the cardiac cycle.

Tissue Elasticity Assessment

Non-invasive evaluation of myocardial elasticity using elastography and computational modeling techniques.

Current Projects

Cardiac Motion Tracking

Advanced algorithms for tracking cardiac motion and deformation throughout the cardiac cycle.

Development

Mechanical Property Prediction

Predictive models for estimating tissue mechanical properties from non-invasive imaging measurements.
