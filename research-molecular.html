Molecular Heart Research

Integration of genomics, proteomics, and biomarker data with imaging for personalized cardiac medicine and precision therapeutic approaches.

Research Focus Areas

Genomics and Cardiac Disease Correlation

Investigation of genetic variants and their associations with cardiac diseases, including genome-wide association studies and polygenic risk scoring.

Proteomics Data Integration

Analysis of protein expression patterns in cardiac tissues and their correlation with disease phenotypes and imaging findings.

Biomarker Discovery and Validation

Identification and validation of novel biomarkers for cardiac disease diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment response monitoring.

Personalised Medicine Approaches

Development of personalized treatment strategies based on multi-omics data integration and patient-specific risk profiling.

Current Projects

Multi-omics Data Fusion

Integration of genomics, proteomics, metabolomics, and imaging data for comprehensive cardiac disease characterization.

In Progress

Genetic Risk Prediction Models

Machine learning models for predicting cardiac disease risk based on genetic and clinical factors.

Active

Precision Therapy Selection

AI-driven platforms for selecting optimal therapeutic interventions based on patient molecular profiles.
